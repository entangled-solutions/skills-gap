About

The heatmap shows the gap the importance of a skill to a job, and how well education prepares students for each of those skills. The skills are sorted by three core categories of skill—analytical, social, and technical. The results illustrate a skills gap for analytical and social skills across nearly all career clusters.

Methodology

The source data for the heatmap is from the Department of Labor’s Occupational Information Network (O*NET). O*NET assigns ratings to each skill as a measure of importance to the occupation. The scale ranges from 1 (not important) to 5 (extremely important).

Entangled Solutions developed a corresponding rating for each career cluster to measure the extent to which postsecondary education prepares learners for that particular skill. The scale ranges from 1 (no preparation) to 5 (extreme preparation). These ratings we applied to every occupation in that cluster.

The skills gap value was calculated by subtracting the job importance value from the education preparation value. A negative value (shown in reds) indicates that education is not adequately preparing students for the respective skill. A positive value (shown in greens) indicates that education is adequately preparing students for the respective skills.
